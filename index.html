About

I am a researcher focusing on program synthesis, vision–language models, inference scaling and diversity, and world models. My work explores robust code generation and semantic parsing, diagnostic benchmarks and decoding strategies for multimodal models, and methods to increase hypothesis diversity in large language models.

Selected Research & Projects

  • Transductive Program Synthesis — a test-time formulation leveraging test inputs for more robust synthesis; validated via programmatic world modeling experiments (NeurIPS 2025).
  • Spurious Program Mitigation for Weakly Supervised Semantic Parsing — algorithm to filter execution-based spurious programs (EMNLP 2023).
  • Measuring Language Priors in VLMs — method & benchmark isolating language priors from confounds (NAACL 2025 Findings).
  • Summary-Guided Decoding for VLMs — reduces hallucination by shortening input context during decoding (NAACL 2025 Findings).
  • Diverse Hypotheses for Inductive Reasoning — increases hypothesis diversity beyond IID sampling (NAACL 2025).
  • World Models — improving dynamics modeling via LLM world knowledge and Bayesian reasoning (work in progress).
